# 07 - Basecamp

Version: 0.0.1
Last updated: 2026-07-07
Author: Dru + GPT
Reason for change: Defines the design philosophy, interaction principles and visual language of North

## Why Basecamp?

Every expedition has a basecamp.

It is not the destination.

It is not where the adventure happens.

It is where you prepare.

Where you recover.

Where you check your equipment.

Where you plan your next move.

North should feel the same.

People do not come to North to stay inside the app.

They come to prepare for the life waiting outside it.

## Design Philosophy

North should never feel like software.

It should feel like a calm place people enjoy returning to.

Every screen should reduce mental load.

Every interaction should feel deliberate.

Every animation should have a purpose.

Nothing exists simply because it looks impressive.

## Design Principles

### Calm

No visual noise.

No flashing.

No clutter.

No unnecessary colours.

The interface should help people breathe, think and focus.

### Confidence

Buttons feel solid.

Typography is bold but elegant.

Information is organised.

Nothing feels uncertain.

### Clarity

The user should never wonder what to press next.

Every action should feel obvious.

### Warmth

Professional, never cold.

Premium, never sterile.

Friendly, never childish.

### Purpose

Every pixel must earn its place.

If removing something improves clarity, remove it.

## Mobile First

North is designed for the phone.

Desktop is an extension, not the other way around.

Every screen should be comfortable to use with one hand.

Thumb-first navigation.

Large touch targets.

Natural gestures.

Minimal typing.

## Dashboard Philosophy

The dashboard belongs to the user.

North never rearranges it automatically.

Users build their own Basecamp.

Everything stays where they left it.

Nova may suggest improvements.

Nova never moves things without permission.

## Layout

Whitespace is intentional, but not excessive.

Information density should be higher than most modern apps without ever feeling crowded.

Every screen should maximise useful information while remaining easy to scan.

## Cards

Cards are the building blocks of North.

Rounded.

Soft shadows.

Subtle borders.

Generous but practical padding.

No heavy outlines.

Cards should feel like physical objects resting on the screen.

## Colour

North supports two modes.

### Night

Deep charcoal.

Soft blacks.

Muted blues.

Warm whites.

Minimal accent colours.

### Dawn

Soft off-white.

Very pale sky blue.

Natural greys.

Gentle contrast.

Never pure white.

Accent colours exist to communicate meaning, not decoration.

## Typography

Typography carries the personality.

Readable first.

Beautiful second.

No novelty fonts.

Large headings.

Comfortable body text.

Excellent spacing.

## Motion

Animation should feel like breathing.

Nothing should snap.

Nothing should bounce unnecessarily.

Everything eases naturally.

Transitions should feel calm, not playful.

## Haptics

Every meaningful interaction should feel tactile.

Completing a workout.

Logging a PR.

Finishing a bike ride.

Checking off today's session.

Gentle feedback.

Never excessive.

## Charts

Charts are one of North's superpowers.

They should feel familiar to anyone who has used financial trading platforms.

Interactive.

Smooth.

Beautiful.

Always explain the story behind the data.

A graph without context is just a picture.

## Photography

Images should feel real.

Real people.

Real progress.

Real effort.

Never unrealistic physiques.

Never shame.

Never perfection.

## Illustration

Illustrations should support understanding.

Not decoration.

Exercise demonstrations.

Technique.

Movement.

Recovery.

## Accessibility

Large text options.

Colour-blind friendly.

Screen reader support.

High contrast mode.

Every interaction should remain elegant.

Accessibility is part of good design, not an afterthought.

## Interaction Principles

North should always feel one tap away.

Never five.

Users should spend less time navigating and more time living.

## Loading

Opening North should feel like arriving somewhere.

A soft fade.

Quiet.

Calm.

The logo appears.

No loud splash screens.

No unnecessary waiting.

Just enough time to take a breath.

## Empty States

An empty screen should never feel empty.

It should feel like an invitation.

Example:

You have not logged your first workout yet.

Let's begin.

## Notifications

North does not interrupt.

North invites.

Examples:

- Fancy a walk?
- Looks like you have had a big week.
- Want to reset together?

## Design Rule

Every screen should answer one question:

Does this reduce friction?

If not, redesign it.

## Basecamp Principle

North is not where life happens.

North is where people prepare to go and live it.

## Founder Note

The design of North should never compete for attention.

It should quietly become one of the most comfortable places a person visits each day.
